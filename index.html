I’m a 4th-year undergraduate pursuing a simultaneous B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering and B.S. in Mathematics. I’ll be applying to Computer Science Ph.D. programs this fall, hoping to specialize in robot learning. I’m also looking for an internship for the summer of 2021 to bridge the gap.

I’m an undergraduate research assistant in the Socially Intelligent Machines Lab. At the moment, I’m working with human-in-the-loop reinforcement learning, specifically integrating (possibly noisy) human feedback with information from environmental rewards in reinforcement learning, under the supervision of Taylor K. Faulkner and Professor Andrea Thomaz. Previously I worked on the expression of teamwork by robotic agents with Mai Lee Chang and built a novel interactive regression debugger with Ben Buhse and Professor Milos Gligoric. I also served as a TA for Professor Christine Julien and Professor Pedro Santacruz’s Algorithms class, a responsibility I greatly enjoyed but unfortunately had difficulty balancing with research and classes.

In my spare time, I enjoy playing volleyball, cooking, playing the piano, and watching anime; bonus points if the anime is about any of the other aforementioned hobbies.
